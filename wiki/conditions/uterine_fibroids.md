# Uterine Fibroids

**First Noted:** 2026-06-06 via [[2026-06-06_USG_Lower_Abdomen]]

## Description
Multiple heterogenous intramural to submucous masses in the anterior and posterior wall of the uterus. 
- Largest mass sizes: 6.1 x 4.9 cm (fundo posterior region) and 4.4 x 3.8 cm (right posterolateral wall).
- The uterus is enlarged (12.5 x 6.8 x 8.6 cm) and its shape is distorted.

## Symptoms & Associations
- **Heavy Menstrual Bleeding (Menorrhagia):** See [[2026-06-06_Mittals_Clinic_Visit]]. This bleeding may be exacerbated or driven by an underlying [[thyroid_disorders.md|Thyroid Disorder]] (Hypothyroidism is strongly linked to heavy bleeding).
- **Secondary Anemia:** See [[anemia.md]].

## Current Management
- **Immediate:** Currently taking Tranexamic Acid and Regestrone. 
> [!CAUTION] 
> **Thrombosis Risk:** The combination of Tranexamic Acid (antifibrinolytic) and Regestrone (progestin) in a patient with a history of [[hypertension.md]] carries a significant risk of blood clots. This must be monitored closely.
- **The "Submucous" Angle:** Because these fibroids bulge into the uterine cavity, they often render a **Mirena IUD** ineffective or highly prone to expulsion.
- **Long-term Guideline:** The consensus for this specific profile (49yo with submucous fibroids causing bleeding) is a **Hysteroscopic Myomectomy** to preserve the uterus and safely shave down the fibroid, bridging to menopause.
- **Diagnostics:** An **Endometrial Biopsy (EMB)** is strongly recommended to rule out malignancy, alongside the ordered `S.TSH` test. 
*(See [[multisystem_bleeding_guidelines.md]] for full research)*
