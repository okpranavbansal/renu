## 5. Important Nuances & "Gotchas" to Watch Out For (Based on Web Research)
- **The Deductible Trap**: This is an *annual aggregate deductible* plan. This means if you have three hospitalizations in a year costing ₹4 Lakhs, ₹3 Lakhs, and ₹2 Lakhs (Total ₹9 Lakhs), Niva Bupa will pay **₹0**, because the total is below the ₹10 Lakh deductible. The policy *only* triggers once your total admissible bills cross ₹10 Lakhs in that specific policy year.
- **Room Rent Limits**: Even though the policy allows a "Single Private Room", hospitals have different tiers of private rooms. Opting for a suite or a premium room higher than the basic "Single Private Room" will lead to a **proportionate deduction**. This means not just the room rent difference, but all associated doctor fees, nursing charges, and OT charges will be reduced proportionately.
- **Pre-Existing Disease (PED) Waiting Period**: The 36-month waiting period is strictly enforced. Any hospital admission related to her declared thyroid or hypertension issues before July 31, 2028, will likely be rejected.
- **Consumables & Non-Medical Items**: Standard insurance exclusions apply to consumables like gloves, masks, admission kits, and dietary supplements unless part of the active treatment room charge. (See Annexure III in the policy document for the full list of excluded items).
