# Multisystem Bleeding & Fibroid Guidelines

This research cache stores clinical guidelines for managing bleeding fibroids in patients with concurrent hypertension and thyroid disorders.

## 1. The Submucous Fibroid Angle
Fibroids that are submucous bulge into the uterine cavity. 
- **Mirena IUD:** Highly prone to expulsion or failure because the fibroid distorts the cavity.
- **Hysteroscopic Myomectomy (TCRF):** The preferred minimally invasive surgical option. It involves shaving down the fibroid through the cervix without abdominal incisions.

## 2. Drug Interaction: Thrombosis Risk
- The patient is taking **Tranexamic Acid** (an antifibrinolytic that prevents clot breakdown) and **Regestrone** (a progestin hormone that increases clot risk).
- In a patient with **Hypertension** (a cardiovascular risk factor), this combination poses a significant risk for thromboembolic events (blood clots). Clinical guidelines advise extreme caution.

## 3. The Thyroid "Root Cause"
- **Hypothyroidism** is a well-documented cause of both heavy menstrual bleeding (menorrhagia) and secondary hypertension.
- Evaluating the thyroid status (via TSH) is crucial, as treating the thyroid may resolve the heavy bleeding and help control blood pressure.
- ACOG guidelines mandate an **Endometrial Biopsy (EMB)** to rule out precancerous changes for any perimenopausal abnormal bleeding.

## Action Plan Summary
1. Aggressive iron supplementation for anemia (Hb 9.4).
2. Follow up on the `S.TSH` test to address the potential thyroid root cause.
3. Monitor closely for signs of thrombosis due to the medication combination + hypertension.
4. If medical management fails, discuss Hysteroscopic Myomectomy instead of an IUD.
